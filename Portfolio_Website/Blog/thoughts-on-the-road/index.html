Aaaand I'm back!

I know, it's been a little more than 2-3 weeks, but what can I say? It's been a busy month!

Let's start from where I left off a month ago... California! I went for a change of scenery to see some old friends and work in a different area, which was a fabulous idea on my part. I also participated in a Spikeball tournament with a good friend... you can watch us play on youtube by clicking here

Additionally, the first picture you see in the carousel is of my friend Josh and myself eating a funnel cake. It is always a blast to see that guy and hopefully will be able to catch him at some other Spikeball tournaments this year!

Another quick life update: I'm starting my masters at Purdue in a week. I know, quick turnaround time. I was originally supposed to start this fall, but managed to talk my way into taking it this summer. The grind starts now.

Lastly in terms of life updates is that I recently traveled to Atlanta to play another tournament. Sadly I didn't take any footage, but it was a blast to play nonetheless. The third picture you see is me and another Michigan native hanging out post-tournament

Here are some thoughts from the past month, in no particular order

  • I need to learn to take more pictures in the moment. Even though I like just remembering things, sometimes I still need to capture the little moment
  • I need to work on my self-talk when in life. When I make mistakes, I am very critical of myself. In the words of many people; "I'm my own worst critic". This is very literal, and the people around me know this the most. It's time to start being forgiving of myself while still maintaining high standards
  • Starting a masters program is a little scary, especially with a full time job. It's entirely possible I will be fully overwhelmed and not know how to handle it. But pressure is not something I'm unused to, and I will find a way to adapt
  • Set unachievable goals: meaning that I should set goals that I am afraid I might not achieve. If I limit myself and only set goals that I know I'll achieve, then I'm not growing. I want to try furthering my comfort zone and making a path of my own design

Overall, this past month has been a great relief from the day-to-day grind of training, working, and self-improvement... but its time to get back to it. See you all soon!
